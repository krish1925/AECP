Adapter Comparison

nDCG@10 Retention measures how well Isotrieve preserves retrieval quality compared to re-embedding everything from scratch.

A retention of 0.866 means Isotrieve achieves 86.6% of the quality you'd get by fully re-embedding. The remaining ~13% is the cost of not re-embedding.

Ridge (linear regression) is the default and best performer. MLP (neural network) does worse — linear mapping is sufficient for this task.

Calibration Size (K)

K is the number of texts you embed with both models to learn the mapping. More texts = better mapping, but more cost.

K=2000 is the minimum for a reliable mapping (PASS verdict). Below that, the mapping is too noisy.

The cost is O(K) API calls — for K=2000, that's ~2000 embeddings, which is cheap compared to re-embedding your entire corpus.

Same-Dimension Pair

Both bge-large and e5-large produce 1024-dimensional vectors. You'd think they'd be compatible — they're not.

Floor = 0.0: Raw cross-space retrieval gets zero results. The vectors are in completely different geometric spaces.

Retention = 0.908: After Isotrieve mapping, we recover 90.8% of the quality you'd get by re-embedding everything.

Ceiling is the best possible (full re-embed). Isotrieve gets close to it without touching your corpus.

Score Recalibration

When you search with mapped vectors, the similarity scores are compressed — they don't span the same range as native scores.

Isotonic regression learns a mapping from compressed scores back to the full range. This makes threshold-based decisions (e.g., "only return results with score > 0.7") reliable again.

Same-dim pairs have mild compression (MAE=0.095) — recalibration helps a little. Rectangular pairs have severe compression (MAE=0.364) — recalibration is essential.
